A History of Surfing

Introduction

Surfing is a surface water sport in which the wave rider, referred to as a surfer, rides on the forward section, or face, of a moving wave, usually carrying the surfer toward the shore. Waves suitable for surfing are primarily found in the ocean, but can also be found in lakes or in man-made wave pools.
